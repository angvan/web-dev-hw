About my collection

I enjoy writing haiku and senryu, and sketching with pencil. If you are uncertain what haiku and senryu are, I will tell you.

Haiku is an ancient form of Japanese poetry containing (in English) a total of 17 syllables shared between three lines. The syllable pattern is 5-7-5, which means 5 syllables for the first line, 7 syllables for the second line, 5 syllables for the third line. Traditionally haiku are about the seasons, nature or the natural world. Of course this is only a brief explanation of haiku.

Senryu is similar to haiku in its syllables and structure, however senryu tends to be more about human foibles and are often darkly humorous or cynical. Below you will find links to sites that provide more information about these two forms of poetry.
